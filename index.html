Introduction To My Inrests

Who am I, and what is this? This is a website to explain to you the reader, who I am and what I do with my free time. Now, let’s start off with who I am. Obviously my name is Alexander, but I have a more intricate past than most would think. I was born in Las Vegas Nevada but moved to New Mexico in a small town where I would spend the rest of my youth. I will say, there’s not a lot for a child to do in New Mexico. So I spend my hours skating, scootering, biking, and even on the trampoline. I often went cliff jumping near my grandmothers house, and when it snowed, I would sled down the steepest hills of Ramah. After moving to a more suburban part of New Mexico, my crave for adrenaline was amped, along with my age. I was constantly outside trying to find fun things that would get my blood pumping. I would explore the tangled underground tunnels that helped with rainfall, and climb tree’s on day’s where I could be outside for hours. My determination to defeat any fear I had in my was taking over at this point I was undeniably a fearless kid. During my teenage years I would move to Pensilvania with my father where I would have to leave behind all my old trophies. I was now left with nothing, no skateboard, scooter, BMX bike, or a trampoline. From there I went through the stages of a kid where you get fat, grow a little and thin out, after I was a primed teenager. I was explosive with energy and I needed to do something instead of sitting inside playing Playstation (I was dirty.) So I finally got another skateboard and was back at it. I also got a snowboard and this added another element to my talents. However, during this time I was also enrolled in a private school where I was rigorously challenged academically and socially as I spend most my time by myself or with my siblings. This is where I had to put down the extreme sports and actually try. I was difficult adjusting, however I used what I learned in my youth and overcame whatever challenged me. I learned new methods to study and became a pretty good student. Entering my high school years, I was forced to play a sport so I would stay active. With great intellect knowing I have little to no muscle on me as well as being 5’3 at the time I chose to row. A year later, realizing that was one of my most regrettable decisions, I quit and started playing rugby where I figured my athletic ability would be more useful. I was correct in this thinking, I won a reward for the best back (position) for our junior varsity team, then was promoted to play varsity the end of my sophomore, junior, and senior year. I may be small, but Im shifty and quick as well as having great hand eye coordination from being a demon with a controller. Our team was so good that during my time playing varsity, only our varsity team was invited to play in New Orleans as well as Ireland. During this era where I was at my peak athletic ability, durability, and stamina, this is when I was also the most engulfed in skating and snowboarding. I was learning new tricks almost every week, hitter bigger spots every time all the way up to a 9 stair (Probably my biggest accomplishment as a 18 year old.) After all this time of skating, snowboarding, flipping, scootering, and biking, I had never gotten hurt besides a bruise. But everyone knows that nothing good lasts forever, then rugby put me out of commission my senior year. I first got a concussion at the end of the first season, so I could not skate or play video games. Then 2 weeks into being cleared to play again, I broke my hand during practice out of all thing. Luckily it wasn’t a horrible break and within three weeks I got it off but was recommended not to play because the bone was fragile. However, I found a love for rugby and was eager to play again already missing three weeks of the season. Also I got my cast off 2 days before our flight to Ireland so I had to play. I was fine throughout the whole trip, of course I felt the effects of having a weak forearm, but I powered through and played my heart out. After this, the very same week we got back, I sprained my ankle. This was one injury that was always so prone to happen from skating but never did. This one really hurt my sole, this is because I couldn’t skate anymore. I had hurt my left ankle which was the one I used to flick/ drag, it was one of the most important aspects of skating, and when it still hurt to land on after 6 months I almost gave up skating. But that’s not who I am, I did ankle strength training and therapy everyday on YouTube. Until finally I was able to painlessly skate again. At this point it had been so long that I had lost so much balance, drop durability, and a few tricks. This one hurt the ego as I was not as good as before but that brings me here where I have been skating at least 4 times a week since welcome week of college. Finally I can experience happiness again.
